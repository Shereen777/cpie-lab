01 / Vision

Bridging the 5–8% gap

The P@SHA report found that only 5–8% of CS graduates are considered employable. CPiE exists to change that — through research, immersive technology, and rethinking how programming is taught.

Computational Thinking

Strengthen problem-solving abilities through competitive programming frameworks and grassroots-level research into programming pedagogy.

Immersive Learning

Pioneering Virtual Reality-based instruction in Pakistan, with active collaborations with Copenhagen Business School, Kristiania University College, and the University of Toronto.

Industry Bridge

Linking academia with DeltaLab, Mindstorm Studio, PITB, dirAction, Takhleeq, Educative, and SeedProgramming to produce industry-ready graduates.

Nano Programs

Scalable training tracks in AI, Data Science, Game Development, and Mathematical Foundations — launching this summer, Insha'Allah.

02 / Research

Research at the Frontier

From immersive VR studies to the foundations of mathematical reasoning in code.

Published · ECIS 2025
A+ Conference · Meta Study

Teaching Programming in VR vs. Online vs. Physical Classrooms

One of the first immersive Virtual Reality–based studies in Pakistan, published at the European Conference on Information Systems in collaboration with Copenhagen Business School and Kristiania University College. Now extended into a longitudinal full-semester study with the University of Toronto.

🇩🇰 Copenhagen Business School 🇳🇴 Kristiania University College 🇨🇦 University of Toronto
Logic · Pedagogy

Quantifiers to Loops

Linking ∃ Existential and ∀ Universal quantifiers in propositional logic to for / while loop constructs — making mathematical reasoning tangible to programmers.

In Progress · Pedagogy

Concept Maps for Intuition

An ongoing exploration into how Concept Maps broaden intuitive thinking — unlocking new methods of teaching that help students visualize abstract relationships in code.

Active

Induction & Inductive Hypothesis

A research paper exploring induction and the inductive hypothesis — and how its rigorous teaching transforms the way students reason about recursion and correctness.

MS Theses

Two Active MS Research Projects

Two ongoing research projects under progress with Master's students — extending CPiE's footprint into deeper, theory-grounded contributions to programming education.

#include <iostream>
using namespace std;

// CodeForge auto-evaluates each test case
template<typename T>
class Stack {
    T* data;
    int top, cap;
public:
    Stack(int c=10) : top(-1), cap(c) {
        data = new T[cap];
    }
    void push(T v) { data[++top] = v; }
    T pop()        { return data[top--]; }
};
